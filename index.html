About

I’m a second-year PhD student in the MESL Lab at UC San Diego, advised by Prof. Rajesh Gupta and Prof. Patrick Pannuto. My work focuses on algorithm–architecture co-design for time series inference, spanning classification and forecasting. I develop energy efficient systems for time series classification while maintaining state of the art accuracy, particularly for applications such as human activity recognition and fall detection on FPGAs and other edge platforms. My current research aims to improve forecasting via co-design to enable faster, more scalable analysis of time series data. My expertise includes RTL design and modeling, FPGA prototyping of hardware accelerators, and low power techniques for ASIC flows.
